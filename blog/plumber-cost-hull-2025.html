How Much Does a Plumber Cost in Hull? (2025 Price Guide)

Transparent pricing for common plumbing jobs in Hull. No surprises, no hidden fees. Here's what you'll actually pay.

Typical plumber callout costs in Hull

Most plumbers in Hull don't charge a separate callout fee anymore. Instead, the first hour's labour is included in your booking. That's the model we use: no callout fee, first hour included, then £85-£110 per hour after that depending on the job complexity.

Emergency callouts outside business hours cost more. Expect to pay £120-£150 for the first hour if you call late evening or weekends. That's standard across Hull and East Yorkshire. If a plumber quotes you the same rate for emergency and standard callouts, they're probably overcharging for standard work.

Some older plumbers still charge a separate callout (£50-£75) plus hourly labour on top. That model is dying out because customers don't like paying just for someone to show up. But you'll still find it with sole traders who've been in the game 20+ years.

Beware of plumbers who quote very low hourly rates but then add charges for everything: waste disposal, cleaning up, travel time, parking, 'specialist' equipment that's actually standard kit. A £50/hour plumber who adds £80 in extras costs more than a £90/hour plumber who includes everything. Always ask for a full breakdown before agreeing.

Common plumbing repairs and what they cost

Dripping tap repair: £70-£120 depending on whether it's a washer replacement or a whole cartridge change. Kitchen mixer tap cartridges are more expensive than basic basin tap washers. Takes 30-45 minutes in most cases.

Toilet repairs: Cistern mechanism replacement is £80-£140. Full toilet replacement including removing the old one and fitting the new one costs £180-£280. That's labour only, the toilet itself is extra if we're supplying it.

Burst pipe repair: £120-£200 depending on location and pipe type. A burst under the kitchen sink is quick to access and fix. A burst in a wall or under a floor takes longer and costs more because of the access work.

Radiator valve replacement: £85-£130 per radiator. That includes draining down the system, replacing the valve, refilling, and bleeding radiators. If multiple radiators need doing, we can do them more efficiently and charge less per valve.

Power flushing: £350-£500 for an average 3-bed semi in Hull. Larger houses with 10+ radiators cost more. It takes 4-6 hours to flush a system properly, and we use professional power flushing equipment, not a garden hose.

Outside tap installation costs £85-£140 for a standard location on an external wall with the cold supply pipe running directly behind. If the nearest cold pipe is across the room or on a different floor, add £60-£120 for the extra pipework. We assess it during the quote visit and give you an exact price before starting.

Boiler repairs and replacement costs Hull

Boiler repairs vary wildly depending on what's failed. Simple fixes like pressure loss or thermostat faults cost £90-£140. Parts like pumps, diverter valves, or PCBs cost £180-£350 including labour. Heat exchanger replacement costs £350-£550 because it's basically a new boiler internally.

Full boiler replacement in Hull costs £1,800-£3,000 including the boiler, flue, controls, and installation. That's for a standard combi boiler in a straightforward swap. If you're changing boiler type (e.g., regular to combi) or moving its location, add £500-£800 for pipework alterations.

We don't recommend repairing boilers over 12-15 years old if the repair costs more than £300. At that age, more parts will fail soon, and you'll keep spending money on an old inefficient boiler. Better to invest in a new one with a 7-10 year warranty.

Annual boiler services cost £70-£95 in Hull. Get it done every year to keep your warranty valid and catch small faults before they become expensive breakdowns. We check all components, test combustion, inspect the heat exchanger, and pressure test the system. Takes about 45 minutes. Book it for September before the winter rush.

Drain clearance costs in Hull

Manual rodding for simple blockages: £80-£120. Takes 30-60 minutes. If we can clear it with rods, that's the cheapest option. Works for most toilet and sink blockages caused by toilet paper, hair, or minor debris buildup.

High-pressure jetting: £130-£200. We use a professional jetter that blasts water at 3,000+ PSI through the drain. This clears grease, fat, tree roots, and stubborn blockages that rods can't shift. Takes 1-2 hours depending on the blockage severity and drain length.

CCTV drain survey: £150-£250. We put a camera down your drain to see exactly what's causing the problem. Essential if you've got recurring blockages or suspect a collapsed pipe. The footage shows cracks, root intrusion, and pipe condition. You get a recording and a written report.

Drain repairs and relining: £400-£1,200+ depending on the damage. Relining costs £400-£800 for a short section and is cheaper than excavation. Excavating and replacing a collapsed drain costs £1,200-£2,500 depending on depth and location.

Manhole cover replacement costs £120-£180 for a standard 600mm circular cover. Larger square covers or heavy-duty sealed covers cost more. If the frame is also damaged, full frame and cover replacement costs £200-£300 including breaking out the old frame and setting the new one in concrete.

What affects plumber pricing in Hull

Time of day makes a big difference. Standard working hours (Mon-Fri 8am-6pm) are cheapest. Evenings, weekends, and bank holidays cost 30-50% more. Christmas Day and New Year's Day, expect to pay double or more if you can find someone willing to work.

Job complexity matters more than duration. Replacing a visible pipe takes less skill than tracing a hidden leak or working in a tight space. You're paying for expertise and problem-solving, not just time spent on site.

Parts costs vary. We carry common parts on the van (washers, valves, connectors) but specialist parts need ordering. If we need to order parts, you'll pay for those separately, plus potentially another callout fee for the return visit unless we waive it.

Your location in Hull affects travel time but not usually the hourly rate. We cover all Hull areas at the same rate. Some plumbers charge extra for Beverley, Cottingham, or Hessle, but we don't as long as it's within HU postcodes.

Plumbers who turn up in branded vans with company logos tend to charge more than sole traders in unmarked vans. The branding costs money, as does advertising, insurance, and administrative overhead. You're paying for that. Doesn't mean they're better plumbers, just that they've got higher business expenses to cover. A good sole trader can be just as skilled and reliable.

How to avoid being overcharged by plumbers

Always ask for a quote before work starts, especially for big jobs. Good plumbers will give you a clear price upfront or explain what the hourly rate is and roughly how long the job will take. If they won't quote, that's a red flag.

Get multiple quotes for expensive work like boiler replacements or bathroom installations. Prices vary a lot between plumbers. Three quotes gives you a sense of the market rate. Cheapest isn't always best, but nor is most expensive.

Don't pay big deposits upfront for materials. A legitimate plumber will buy materials on account and bill you later or ask for a small deposit (10-20%) if it's a custom order. Anyone asking for 50%+ upfront before starting work might not be trustworthy.

Check what's included in the quote. Does it include materials? Making good walls and floors after repairs? VAT? Testing and certification? A low quote that excludes everything might end up costing more than a higher quote that includes it all.

Ask if the plumber is Gas Safe registered if they're working on boilers or gas pipework. It's illegal to work on gas without being registered. Check their card and look them up on the Gas Safe website. If they're not registered, walk away no matter how cheap they are.

For complex jobs like bathroom installations or full heating systems, get three quotes minimum and check reviews online. Ask for references from recent customers. Anyone who's done good work will happily put you in touch with past customers. If they refuse or make excuses, that's a warning sign.

How to find a reliable plumber in Hull

Start with recommendations from neighbors, friends, or family who've had plumbing work done recently. Personal recommendations are more reliable than online reviews, which can be faked or biased. Ask what the plumber was like, did they turn up on time, quote accurately, clean up afterward.

Check online reviews on Google, Trustpilot, or Checkatrade, but read them critically. Look for patterns in negative reviews. One person complaining might just be unreasonable. Ten people saying the plumber didn't turn up means there's a problem. Beware of plumbers with only 5-star reviews and no critical feedback, it might be fake.

Verify credentials before hiring. Gas Safe registration is legally required for gas work. Check the plumber's Gas Safe card and look them up on the Gas Safe website to confirm it's valid. For water work, there's no legal registration requirement, but membership of trade bodies like CIPHE or WaterSafe indicates professional standards.

Get quotes in writing. A verbal quote is worthless if there's a dispute. Ask for a written quote that includes labour, materials, VAT, and any extras. If the final bill is significantly higher than the quote without good reason (e.g., unexpected extra work agreed in writing), that's grounds for disputing it.

Never pay the full amount upfront. Legitimate plumbers don't demand full payment before starting. They might ask for a deposit for expensive materials, but 10-20% is reasonable, not 50%+. Pay on completion when you're satisfied with the work.
